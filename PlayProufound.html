Play Profound Paper

Sabrina Achcar-Winkels

22 November, 2021

    Susan Rethorst sees choreography and dancing, not as an expression of necessarily neatly
formed ideas, but as a means of discovering things that you didn’t know you knew. It’s about
finding your own voice. We tend to go with what we already know, the textbook versions of work.
Follow by the rules that were taught to use, being afraid to go outside those boundaries.
Rethorst had to excavate from the myths of making, and liberated herself to be able to go out of
the bounds when it came to choreography and dancing.
    This feeling of being bound to one thing and one specific way of thinking is relatable
within the design world. Such as Rethorst, we’re taught to design a certain way and then we’re
assigned to create these projects that follow the guidelines of what the professor expects. Being
told what and what not to do strains creativity. We are being told to use our minds to make
something wonderful, but we’re also being held from using our uniqueness and creative minds.
    Rethorst used a lot of outside influence to gain that sense of independence, steering away
from the myths of dance. Realizing that you don’t have to wait around for some inspiration. She
began to take a class in the 70s called ‘A Dance A Day’, where you show up to class and create a
new choreographed dance. It was immediate and terrifying, but it got Rethorst and other students
to begin using movement in ways that they hadn’t before. You show up and you dance, making a
dance everyday.
    This idea of making something new everyday, without much thought, is exactly what
designers attempt to do to get out of our own heads. Rethorst had to create a dance a day,
whereas myself and other designers can create a design a day, with no restrictions. Being able to l
et your creative mind free of restricts, is both exciting and challenging. Not being told what to
do or how to do it isn’t something that we are used to. We’ve been trained to follow orders and
we do as we are told. Being able to open up to endless possibilities without having to wait for
inspiration is tough, but it’s the only way to open up a new window of creativeness and
uniqueness.
